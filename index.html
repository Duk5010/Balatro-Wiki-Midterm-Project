Welcome to the Balatro Wiki

Balatro is a critically acclaimed roguelike deckbuilder designed by LocalThunk and published by Playstack. Released in February 2024, the game quickly became a hit for its innovative gameplay that combines poker mechanics with roguelike elements and deckbuilding strategy.

"Balatro is a deck-building roguelike where you must play poker hands and earn chips to defeat enemy blinds. Enhance your deck of playing cards, buy Jokers to modify each hand you play, and discover new and wild synergies to win!" ~Official Steam description

What Makes Balatro Special?

Unlike traditional card games, Balatro uses poker hands as a foundation but transforms the experience with special Joker cards that provide unique effects and multipliers. Players build their deck by collecting cards, Jokers, and other items as they progress through increasingly difficult rounds called "Antes."

Game Overview

In Balatro, you start with a standard 52-card deck and must create poker hands (pairs, straights, flushes, etc.) to defeat enemies called "Stakes." As you progress, you'll collect Joker cards that provide powerful effects and multipliers, enhancing your poker hands and allowing you to achieve increasingly higher scores.

Strategic Depth

Endless Possibilities

With over 100 unique Jokers, dozens of Tarot and Planet cards, and multiple playable characters, each run offers a new experience and strategic challenge.

Roguelike Elements

Permadeath & Progression

Each run is unique, with permadeath and procedurally generated content. However, successful runs unlock new characters and game modes.

Accessibility

Easy to Learn

Basic poker knowledge helps, but the game includes comprehensive tutorials and is accessible to newcomers while offering depth for veterans.

Game History

Balatro was developed by solo developer LocalThunk (the pseudonym of game designer Ezra "Chevy" Ray) over approximately two years. The game was initially released on February 20, 2024, and quickly garnered critical acclaim for its innovative mechanics and addictive gameplay. Within its first week, Balatro sold over 250,000 copies across all platforms.

Did You Know?

The word "Balatro" comes from Latin, referring to a professional jester or fool – fitting for a game centered around Joker cards!
